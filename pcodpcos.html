Understanding PCOD

Polycystic Ovarian Disease (PCOD) is characterized by hormonal imbalance. Key points:

  • Symptoms include irregular periods, excessive hair growth, and weight gain.
  • PCOD is often associated with insulin resistance.
  • Hormonal imbalance leading to irregular periods.
  • May cause weight gain, acne, and hair thinning.
  • More common in women aged 12-45 years.

References: Healthline - PCOD, NCBI - PCOD Overview

Understanding PCOS

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder. Key insights:

  • Symptoms include irregular menstrual cycles and acne.
  • PCOS can lead to serious health issues.
  • Increased levels of androgens (male hormones).
  • Irregular or absent menstrual cycles.
  • Can lead to infertility and metabolic issues such as insulin resistance.

References: WomensHealth.gov - PCOS, NCBI - PCOS Management
